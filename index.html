01 — EGC Templates

5 templates that turn employees into prolific content creators.

Each template includes a copy-paste prompt for Claude Cowork and step-by-step instructions. Your team doesn't need to be "good at content" — the AI does the heavy lifting while their authentic voice shines through. Click to expand.

How to Use
Create a Claude Cowork project called "EGC — [Employee Name] Thought Leadership".
Upload: the employee's LinkedIn profile, 2-3 past posts they liked, their speaking topics, any talks/podcasts they've done, and your company's messaging guidelines.
Have a 15-minute voice note conversation with the employee about what they're seeing in their field. Upload the transcript.
Paste the prompt below. Claude will generate a month of content in their voice.
Employee reviews and adds personal anecdotes or tweaks — 10 minutes per post. Authenticity preserved, effort eliminated.
Copy-Paste Prompt
You are a ghostwriting strategist for Employee-Generated Content (EGC). Your job is to help this employee become a recognized thought leader on LinkedIn — using THEIR authentic voice, experiences, and perspective. Review the employee's profile, past content, and voice notes in this project. Mirror their natural tone exactly — don't make them sound like a marketer. **EMPLOYEE NAME:** [Name] **ROLE:** [e.g. "VP of Engineering at [Company]"] **EXPERTISE AREAS:** [e.g. "Scaling engineering teams, developer experience, AI in production"] **VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:** [e.g. "Direct, uses analogies, slightly contrarian, no jargon"] **COMPANY GTM GOAL:** [e.g. "Position us as the leader in AI-powered developer tools"] --- Generate a 4-week LinkedIn content calendar (3 posts/week = 12 posts): ## Week 1: Establish Expertise Post 1 — HOT TAKE: A contrarian opinion based on their real experience Post 2 — STORY: A specific moment/failure/lesson from their career Post 3 — FRAMEWORK: A practical how-to from their domain expertise ## Week 2: Build Trust Post 4 — BEHIND THE SCENES: What their team is actually working on (transparency) Post 5 — DATA POINT: An insight from their work backed by a real number Post 6 — OPINION: React to an industry trend with a strong point of view ## Week 3: Drive Engagement Post 7 — QUESTION POST: Ask their audience something they genuinely want to know Post 8 — MYTH-BUSTING: "Everyone thinks X. Here's why that's wrong." Post 9 — TACTICAL: Step-by-step breakdown of something they do well ## Week 4: Convert Post 10 — CASE STUDY: A win their team/product delivered (subtle company mention) Post 11 — PREDICTION: Where their industry is heading (thought leadership) Post 12 — CTA POST: Soft pitch wrapped in genuine value (ties to company GTM) FOR EACH POST provide: - Full post text (hook line + body + CTA/engagement prompt) - Best day/time to post - Suggested visual (screenshot, chart, photo idea) - Engagement strategy (who to tag, which comments to reply to) CRITICAL: Every post must sound like it was written by the employee, not by marketing. Use first person. Include imperfections. Reference specific experiences from their voice notes. The goal is authentic thought leadership that happens to align with the company's GTM — NOT branded content that sounds corporate.
💡 EGC Tip: Run this for 3-5 employees simultaneously in separate Cowork projects. Within 30 days you'll have 36-60 pieces of authentic content hitting LinkedIn from real voices — that's more reach than most brand accounts get in a quarter.

Copy-Paste Prompt
You are a GTM strategist specializing in Employee-Generated Content campaigns. Build a campaign where employees — not the brand account — are the primary distribution and trust-building channel. **CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE:** [e.g. "Launch new product tier — drive 200 qualified demos in 60 days"] **TARGET ICP:** [e.g. "VP Engineering, Series B-D companies, 100-500 employees"] **KEY MESSAGE:** [e.g. "The only AI workspace built for teams, not individuals"] **EMPLOYEE CONTENT CREATORS:** [List 3-5 employees with their roles and audience size] **BUDGET:** [e.g. "$30K for paid amplification of top-performing EGC"] **TIMELINE:** [e.g. "8 weeks"] --- ## 1. EGC Campaign Strategy - Why employee voices will outperform brand content for this campaign - Content pillar framework: map each employee to a specific angle based on their expertise - Sequencing: how employee posts build on each other to create a narrative wave ## 2. Employee Content Assignments For each employee, generate: - Their specific angle/pillar (what makes them the right voice for this) - 4-6 content pieces across the campaign timeline - Full draft for each post in their unique voice - Personal story hooks that tie to the campaign message - Suggested interactions: who they should engage with, which posts to comment on ## 3. Amplification Strategy - Which EGC posts to boost with paid spend (criteria for selection) - Brand account's role: reshare, comment, and amplify — never lead - Sales team enablement: how reps use EGC posts in outreach sequences - Email integration: EGC content repurposed into nurture campaigns ## 4. Coordination Calendar - Week-by-week timeline: who posts what, when - Engagement cascades: when Employee A posts, Employees B and C engage within 1 hour - Brand account amplification schedule - Paid boost triggers (when a post hits X engagement, boost it) ## 5. Measurement Framework - Per-employee metrics: impressions, engagement rate, profile visits, connection requests - Campaign-level: pipeline influenced by EGC, demos attributed to employee content - EGC vs. brand content comparison (track both, prove the ROI) - Employee advocacy score: measure participation and quality ## 6. Employee Enablement Kit - 1-page guide for each employee: "Here's your role in this campaign" - Do's and don'ts for authenticity (never say "we" when you mean "the company") - Response templates for common comments and DMs - Escalation path for sensitive engagement
💡 EGC Tip: The magic is in the "engagement cascade." When 5 employees post about the same theme in the same week, and engage with each other's content, LinkedIn's algorithm treats it as a trending topic in your ICP's feed. It's organic reach that money can't buy.

Copy-Paste Prompt
You are a content strategist who specializes in converting customer insights into authentic Employee-Generated Content. Analyze all customer data in this project and generate employee-ready content. **DATA SOURCES:** [List what you uploaded] **EMPLOYEE CONTENT CREATORS:** [List employees with roles — e.g. "Sarah (CS Lead), Marcus (Head of Eng), Priya (Product Manager)"] **COMPANY:** [Brief description] **GTM PRIORITY:** [What you're trying to drive] --- ## 1. Customer Insight Extraction - Top 10 customer stories/moments worth sharing (ranked by emotional resonance and GTM relevance) - For each: the raw insight, why it matters, and which employee should tell this story - Verbatim quotes that are gold (with permission guidance) ## 2. Employee Content Pieces (Generate 3 per employee) For each piece: - **Source insight:** Which customer data point inspired this - **Assigned employee:** Who tells this story and why they're the right voice - **Format:** Story post / lesson post / behind-the-scenes / data insight - **Full draft:** Written in that specific employee's voice and perspective - Must connect customer insight → employee's personal experience → broader lesson - Include a specific moment or anecdote that makes it feel real - End with engagement hook (question, hot take, or invitation) - **Suggested visual:** Screenshot, chart, or photo idea - **Compliance note:** What can/can't be shared (customer names, data, etc.) ## 3. Content-to-Funnel Mapping - Which pieces target top-of-funnel (awareness/trust)? - Which target mid-funnel (consideration/proof)? - Which target bottom-funnel (decision/urgency)? - How each piece connects to the next step in the buyer journey ## 4. Repurposing Guide For each employee piece, generate: - A company blog snippet (different angle, links back to employee's post) - An email nurture paragraph - A sales outreach snippet (for reps to reference in cold outreach) - A short-form video script (employee telling the story on camera, 60 seconds) ## 5. Engagement Playbook - How the team should engage with comments on these posts - Suggested replies for common questions - When to pull customers into the conversation (with their permission) - How to turn high-engagement posts into follow-up content
💡 EGC Tip: The best EGC doesn't talk about your product. It talks about the customer's problem — from the perspective of the employee who helped solve it. "I spent 3 hours on a call with a customer who was about to churn..." is 10x more powerful than any case study PDF.

04 — 7-Day EGC Launch Plan

Go from zero to a live EGC program in one week.

A day-by-day plan with specific tasks. By Day 7, your employees are creating content that powers your GTM.

Day 1 — Select & Structure
Choose Your First EGC Creators & Build the Workspace
Start with 3-5 employees who have domain expertise and willingness (not follower count).
✓Identify 3-5 employees across different functions (eng, product, CS, sales, leadership)
✓Create a Claude Cowork project for each employee: "EGC — [Name]"
✓Create a shared project: "EGC Program Hub" (for coordination and analytics)
✓Upload company messaging guidelines, brand voice doc, and ICP research to the Hub
Day 2 — Extract & Profile
Capture Each Employee's Unique Voice and Expertise
This is the most important step. Garbage in, garbage out. Good voice notes in, magic out.
✓Run 15-min voice note sessions with each employee: "What do you know that most people don't?"
✓Upload transcripts + their LinkedIn profiles + any past content to their individual projects
✓Upload top-performing EGC examples from your industry (for format inspiration)
✓Run Template 5 (Onboarding Kit) for each employee — personalized to their voice
Day 3 — Generate
Create the First Month of Content for Every Employee
This is where the leverage kicks in. One afternoon, 60+ pieces of content.
✓Run Template 1 (Thought Leadership Engine) for each employee
✓Review outputs — flag any posts that need more personal anecdotes
✓Share drafts with each employee for personalization (they add 2-3 sentences per post)
✓Optimize LinkedIn profiles using the onboarding kit recommendations
Day 4 — Coordinate
Build the Engagement Cascade & Publishing Calendar
Individual posts are good. Coordinated EGC is a force multiplier.
✓Map out the 4-week publishing calendar in the EGC Program Hub
✓Set up engagement cascades: when Employee A posts, Employees B+C engage within 1 hour
✓Brief the sales team: "Here's what's coming — here's how to use it in outreach"
✓Create a shared Slack channel or notification system for "Employee X just posted — go engage"
Day 5 — Launch
First Posts Go Live
Start with the introduction posts — employees announcing their new content focus.
✓Each employee publishes Post #1 (the introduction/hot take)
✓Execute the engagement cascade — team engages within 60 minutes
✓Monitor comments and help employees respond (use Claude for reply suggestions)
✓Celebrate wins in the team channel — screenshot impressions, share positive comments
Day 6 — Measure
First Performance Check & Quick Wins
48 hours of data is enough to spot early patterns.
✓Pull analytics for all Day 5 posts
✓Run Template 4 (Performance Analyzer) on early data
✓Identify which posts over-performed — create more in that style immediately
✓Adjust tomorrow's posts based on learnings
Day 7 — Systematize
You Have a Running EGC Program. Now Make It Permanent.
The system is live. Lock in the habits that make it self-sustaining.
✓Set up a bi-weekly "EGC review" using Template 4 (15 minutes, data-driven)
✓Create a monthly voice note cadence — 15 minutes per employee to fuel new content
✓Connect the VoC pipeline (Template 3) so customer insights auto-feed into EGC
✓Plan to add 2-3 new employees to the program next month
✓Share the first week's results with leadership — the ROI case makes itself
